Profile

Where do you work as a hospitality/bakery entrepreneur? With the hospitality/bakery entrepreneur diploma you can work in the hospitality and bakery sector.


You can choose to graduate from the program as Manager/Entrepreneur Catering, Manager/Entrepreneur Café-Bar, Manager/Entrepreneur Fast Service or Entrepreneur Bakery.


As a Manager/Entrepreneur Catering you work in a hotel or restaurant. There are stand-alone restaurants and stand-alone hotels, but there are also combinations of hotel and restaurant. You receive guests in the hotel or restaurant and you provide service to the guests.


You mainly offer drinks (such as cocktails) and (small) food items. You provide a cozy and safe atmosphere, so that guests like to come to you. You often work in the evenings and sometimes also at night.


The café-bar manager/entrepreneur at work As a fast service manager/entrepreneur, you work in a fast service company, such as a cafeteria, ice cream parlour, sandwich shop, hamburger chain or pizza takeaway shop.


You sell and prepare fast service products and drinks. Guests usually take their order with them or have it delivered.


You can own a fast service company yourself, but you can also manage a branch that is not your own. In that case you are not solely responsible for the establishment. You can have one or more branches.


If the company consists of several branches, as an entrepreneur you will employ branch managers or managers per branch. As an entrepreneur in the traditional bakery you are responsible for the bakery, the store where the products are sold and the office. You often work (very) early in the morning and in the afternoon.


The bakery entrepreneur at work What kind of work do you do as a hospitality/bakery entrepreneur? With the hospitality/bakery entrepreneur diploma you are qualified to start a business. As a hospitality/bakery entrepreneur, you are primarily an entrepreneur of one or more companies.


In many cases you will own the company (or companies). The size of the company and the number of companies/establishments mean that as an entrepreneur you are more or less involved in the operational management and making sure that things run smoothly in daily practice.Being a catering/bakery entrepreneur in large catering companies is different.


As a hospitality/bakery entrepreneur, you are not the owner of the company (or companies), but you manage a company/establishment. In large companies, you are the person who deals with the positioning of the company in the market. Improving this market position also has your constant attention. You are responsible for drawing up the various plans necessary for running the company.


You determine the policy and also ensure that this policy is implemented. In addition, you take care of business operations and you ensure that all legal rules and regulations related to the company are observed. As a manager/entrepreneur catering, manager/entrepreneur fast service and manager/entrepreneur café-bar you also play an important role in creating and monitoring the atmosphere in the company.
Managers come in many shapes and sizes. What the work of a manager looks like depends on the type of company, the place and the level within that company for which a manager is appointed.

What are you good at as a hospitality/bakery entrepreneur? As a hospitality/bakery entrepreneur you are enthusiastic and driven for the company to be successful. Commercial and business insight is also very important.


You must imagine the following to the Catering/Bakery Entrepreneur: You indicate which way the company should go. You are an innovator; you are open to new developments and develop groundbreaking ideas.


You dare to take risks and can act in uncertain situations. You are guest/customer-oriented and market-oriented. you have good communication skills. You are a networker. You are result-oriented and you can organize well. production. A connection with the industry in which you will be working is also desirable.

Feature qualification files The qualifications Bakery Entrepreneur, Catering Manager/Entrepreneur, Café-Bar Manager/Entrepreneur and Fast Service Manager/Entrepreneur are part of the qualification files (schedule November 2010).
